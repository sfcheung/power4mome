## How is Being "Close Enough" Defined

Being "Close enough" is defined by
the tolerance value, which is determined
internally based on the number of replications (`final_nrep`).
For a smaller value of `final_rep`, the tolerance will
be larger, and so a sample with estimated power
farther away from the target power would be
considered as "close enough." For a `final_nrep`
of 2000, the default tolerance is
`r sprintf("%4.3f", out_n$n_from_power$power_tolerance)`,
which should be practically precise enough.
